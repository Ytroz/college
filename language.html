About Our Program

Preserving Cultural Heritage

Our Language and Tradition program at YTROZ College is dedicated to the study and preservation of Nigeria's rich linguistic and cultural heritage. We offer comprehensive courses in four major Nigerian languages: Yoruba, Igbo, Hausa, and Ijebu.

Students will gain not only language proficiency but also deep understanding of the traditions, customs, folklore, and history associated with each language group.

Yoruba Language & Culture

The Yoruba language is spoken by over 40 million people in Nigeria and in diaspora communities around the world. Our program covers:

  • Yoruba grammar and syntax
  • Traditional Yoruba poetry (Ewi) and proverbs (Owe)
  • Yoruba history and kingdoms
  • Traditional religion and Ifá divination system
  • Yoruba music, dance, and festivals
Common Yoruba Phrases

Hello: Ẹ n lẹ (Ẹ n lẹ o for elders)

How are you?: Ṣe daadaa ni?

Thank you: Ẹ ṣe

Goodbye: O dabo

Welcome: Ẹ ku abọ

Igbo Language & Culture

The Igbo language is spoken by over 30 million people primarily in southeastern Nigeria. Our program covers:

  • Igbo grammar and dialects
  • Igbo proverbs (Ilu) and folktales (Ifo)
  • Igbo-Ukwu art and archaeology
  • Traditional political systems and the Igbo republic
  • New Yam Festival (Iri Ji) and other cultural celebrations
Common Igbo Phrases

Hello: Ndewo

How are you?: Kedu ka ị mere?

Thank you: Daalụ

Goodbye: Ka ọ dị

Welcome: Nnọọ

Hausa Language & Culture

Hausa is the most widely spoken language in West Africa, with over 70 million speakers. Our program covers:

  • Hausa grammar and Ajami script
  • Hausa poetry (Wakoki) and storytelling
  • History of Hausa kingdoms and the Sokoto Caliphate
  • Traditional Hausa architecture and city-states
  • Durbar festival and other cultural celebrations
Common Hausa Phrases

Hello: Sannu

How are you?: Kana lahiya?

Thank you: Na gode

Goodbye: Sai an jima

Welcome: Barka da zuwa

Ijebu Language & Culture

The Ijebu dialect is a distinct variety of Yoruba spoken in southwestern Nigeria. Our program covers:

  • Ijebu phonology and unique vocabulary
  • Differences between Ijebu and Standard Yoruba
  • History of the Ijebu kingdom
  • Ijebu traditional religion and practices
  • Ijebu-Ode festival and other cultural events
Common Ijebu Phrases

Goodbye: O dabo

Welcome: Ẹ ku abọ
