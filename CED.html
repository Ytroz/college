About Our Program

Our Childhood Education program at Ytroz College prepares you to become an innovative and compassionate educator for children from birth through age 8. Through a combination of theory and hands-on experience, you'll develop the skills needed to create engaging, developmentally appropriate learning environments.

The program emphasizes child development, curriculum planning, assessment strategies, and family engagement. Graduates are prepared for careers in preschools, childcare centers, elementary schools, and other early childhood settings.

Accredited by the National Association for Childhood Education
Hands-on field experience starting in your first year
Small class sizes with personalized attention

Program Highlights

Child Development

Comprehensive study of physical, cognitive, social, and emotional development from birth through age 8.

Curriculum Design

Learn to create developmentally appropriate curricula that engage young learners across all domains.

Family Engagement

Strategies for building strong partnerships with families to support children's learning and development.

Field Experience

Over 300 hours of supervised field experience in diverse early childhood settings.

Program Curriculum

Core Courses

Child Growth and Development

Examines typical and atypical development from conception through middle childhood.

Early Childhood Curriculum

Methods for planning and implementing developmentally appropriate curriculum.

Language and Literacy Development

Strategies for fostering emergent literacy skills in young children.

Math and Science for Young Children

Hands-on approaches to teaching foundational math and science concepts.

Specialization Courses

Inclusive Practices

Creating learning environments that support all children, including those with special needs.

Observation and Assessment

Techniques for documenting and assessing children's learning and development.

Play and Learning

The role of play in children's cognitive, social, and emotional development.

Leadership in Early Childhood

Developing leadership skills for advocacy and program administration.
