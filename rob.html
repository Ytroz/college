Course Structure

Our 4-year Bachelor's program is designed to provide comprehensive knowledge in all aspects of robotic engineering:

Year 1: Foundations

Mathematics for Engineers, Physics, Introduction to Programming, Mechanical Design Principles, Electrical Circuits

Year 2: Core Concepts

Control Systems, Digital Electronics, Computer-Aided Design, Sensors and Actuators, Data Structures

Year 3: Specialization

Robot Kinematics, Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, Computer Vision, Embedded Systems

Year 4: Capstone

Advanced Robotics Projects, Industry Internship, Research Thesis, Entrepreneurship in Robotics

Key Modules

Robot Mechanics

Study of robotic movement, dynamics, and mechanical design principles for robotic systems.

Autonomous Systems

Design and programming of robots capable of performing tasks without human intervention.

Human-Robot Interaction

Exploring how humans and robots can work together effectively and safely.

Robotic Vision

Computer vision techniques applied to robotics for object recognition and navigation.

Swarm Robotics

Coordinated behavior of multiple robots working together to accomplish complex tasks.

Ethics in Robotics

Examining the societal impact and ethical considerations of robotic technology.
